About Benign Liver Tumors

Hepatocellular Adenoma

Causes: Often linked to hormonal influences, particularly estrogen from oral contraceptives.

Statistics: Primarily affects women of childbearing age (20-44 years).

Prevention/Management: Discontinuing oral contraceptives can sometimes lead to regression. Monitoring is key.

Hemangioma

Causes: A benign tangle of blood vessels, considered a congenital malformation. The exact cause is unknown.

Statistics: The most common benign liver tumor, more frequently diagnosed in women than men, typically between ages 30-50.

Prevention/Management: Generally requires no treatment unless it becomes large and causes symptoms.

Focal Nodular Hyperplasia (FNH)

Causes: Believed to be a regenerative response of liver cells to a localized vascular abnormality.

Statistics: The second most common benign liver tumor, also more common in women, usually found in adults aged 20-50.

Prevention/Management: Typically requires no treatment and is considered harmless.
